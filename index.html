About Me

I am a second-year MA student in Translation Studies at the Graduate Institute of Interpretation and Translation (GIIT), Shanghai International Studies University, under the supervision of Associate Professor Zhang Ying. I expect to graduate in June 2027.

My research primarily focuses on the History of Translation, with an initial specialization in the English translation history of Chinese Traditional Opera (specifically Yuan Zaju). I investigate the distinct characteristics of Yuan Zaju as it entered the Anglosphere across various historical periods, exploring the underlying causalities linked to the evolution of socio-political landscapes and poetics in the target culture. Heavily influenced by the Cultural School of translation studies, my academic foundation was shaped at the Graduate School of Interpretation and Translation (GSIT), Shanghai International Studies University. My approach aligns with the lineage of Professor Xie Tianzhen, advocating for a shift from prescriptive studies to descriptive translation studies (DTS) and the sociocultural interpretation of the target context, a transition synchronized with the global development of the discipline.

In the era of rapid digital and AI transformation—which coincided with the commencement of my graduate studies—I have embraced the integration of digital humanities with traditional humanistic inquiry. While valuing classical methodologies, I emphasize the necessity of empirical research paradigms in the contemporary age. Moving beyond the corpus-based qualitative research that emerged in the 1990s, I am pioneering the application of Social Network Analysis (SNA) within the field of translation history, particularly in the context of Mainland China. By conceptualizing translation history as a collective of all translation-related social relations, I utilize SNA to uncover macro-phenomena inaccessible through conventional close reading, thereby practicing what Franco Moretti terms "Distant Reading." Furthermore, I am actively exploring the application of Python-based text mining and advanced corpus technologies.

Beyond translation per se, my scholarly interests extend to Political History, specifically the Gengzi Qinvang (Loyalist) Movement and the Baohuang Hui (Chinese Empire Reform Association) during the late Qing Dynasty and early Republican period. My research delves into the translation-related events within the Baohuang Hui, beginning with the identification of translators surrounding Kang Youwei and expanding into an investigation of their identities and ideological stances. I aim to elucidate the pivotal role of translation within this historical movement. I believe that this interdisciplinary approach not only enriches translation history but also offers a unique "translational lens" to the broader field of historiography, revealing nuanced historical dimensions that traditional historical narratives might overlook.
